Martha Ortiz

Martha has a sensitive vision of life, from which she invents new universes. Researcher and connoisseur of social reality (a subject that she studied professionally), she has a deep love for our country and its culture. In her daily work, she has managed to combine both aspects, that of creation, imagination, the discovery of beautiful novelties, on the one hand, and that of flavors and customs inscribed in Mexican roots. Thus, she has to her credit several cookbooks, as well as an important participation in international festivals and congresses. The trajectory of Martha Ortiz Chapa shines with its own light in an essential field of that past and always present history of the country, which is her gastronomy. But in her case it is not a conventional work or kitchen. She inhabits the world of Mexican cuisine to enjoy all the imaginable pleasures that it entails, and not only in the inexhaustible terrain of the senses but also in that of its development and its meaning. The cook loves talking stories with her recipes and she loves visiting markets, as well as admiring the most important haute couture collections in the world. She walks through museums and enjoys reading, late at night, to keep in mind the pictorial in the kitchen and the flavors in the word, which is reflected in the titles of her dishes-stories, the great staging . In this way, she is both an expert in gastronomic art and an informed and imaginative interpreter of our flavors and traditions. Evidence of such richness is the premises of the Dulce Patria restaurant —lit with flavor, seasoning and aroma— that Martha Ortiz Chapa runs successfully in Mexico City and which was recently included in the list of Acqua Panna and St. Pellegrino “Latin America's 50 Best Restaurants 2015 ″, as well as various awards and recognitions among which is the mention of Forbes Mexico, last February, as one of the 30 promises of businesses in our country.

Xchel are the modernist expressions of traditional cuisine. The natural generosity of Mexico is the inspiration of chef Martha Ortiz. Her art is a response to the colors, textures, stories and spirit of her beloved country.
